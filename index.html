The Urgency of Hemorrhagic Shock

“Hemorrhage remains the leading cause of preventable death on the battlefield.”

— COL Brian Eastridge, MD, Joint Trauma System

What Is Hemorrhagic Shock?

Hemorrhagic shock occurs when a person loses so much blood that the heart can no longer supply oxygen to vital organs. Without immediate intervention, the body shuts down within minutes.

Why It Is the Leading Cause of Preventable Battlefield Death

  • • Internal bleeding can be invisible
  • • Clots break apart during movement, stress, or flight
  • • Transfusions may only temporarily stabilize
  • • Shock can return suddenly during MEDEVAC or surgery

Over 90% of survivable combat deaths result from uncontrolled hemorrhage.

A Problem Recognized for Generations

150+ YEARS OF KNOWING THE PROBLEM

From the Civil War to Iraq and Afghanistan, battlefield surgeons have known the same truth: uncontrolled bleeding is the deadliest battlefield injury.

Despite better tourniquets, blood products, and evacuation systems, hemorrhagic shock remains unsolved.

Why Traditional Research Hasn’t Solved It

  • • Cannot ethically induce shock in humans
  • • Cannot replicate chaotic battlefield conditions
  • • Trauma patients cannot safely provide enough data
  • • Clinical research moves in years — shock evolves in minutes

Modern medicine still lacks a real-time model to predict and treat shock early.

How Operation Lifeblood Is Changing Shock Care

Cardiac Digital Twin

A first-ever high-fidelity model of the heart and circulation during blood loss, enabling prediction, simulation, and real-time personalized treatment.

Faster Translation

Turning digital-twin insights into protocols, tools, and training for medics, first responders, and trauma surgeons.

Trauma Innovation

Accelerating promising ideas in shock reversal, diagnostics, and therapeutics that would otherwise never reach the field.

Who We Serve

Military Medicine

First Responders

Military-grade innovations adapted for EMS, fire-rescue, and disaster response.

Trauma Medicine

Partnering with trauma centers and clinicians to integrate digital twins and new standards of care at the bedside.
