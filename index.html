The Urgency of Hemorrhagic Shock

“Hemorrhage remains the leading cause of preventable death on the battlefield.”

— COL Brian Eastridge, MD, Joint Trauma System

What Is Hemorrhagic Shock?

Hemorrhagic shock happens when a person loses so much blood that the heart can’t pump enough to keep the brain and vital organs alive. Without rapid intervention—stopping the bleeding and restoring circulation—the body shuts down in minutes.

Why It Is the Leading Cause of Preventable Battlefield Death

On today’s battlefield, most deaths occur before the casualty reaches a surgeon. When blood loss is severe:

  • • Bleeding can be internal and invisible
  • • Clots break apart during movement or stress
  • • Even transfusion may only temporarily stabilize the patient
  • • Shock can return suddenly during MEDEVAC or early surgery

Because of these challenges, over 90% of potentially survivable combat deaths are caused by uncontrolled hemorrhage.

A Problem Recognized for Generations

Battlefield surgeons have known for over 150 years—from the Civil War through Iraq and Afghanistan—that uncontrolled bleeding kills more Soldiers than any other battlefield injury.

Despite major advances in tourniquets, blood products, and evacuation, hemorrhagic shock remains an unsolved problem.

Why Traditional Research Hasn’t Solved It

Conventional medical research relies on slow, linear clinical trials that:

  • • Can’t simulate the chaos of battlefield conditions
  • • Cannot ethically expose humans to life-threatening shock
  • • Require far more data than trauma patients can safely provide
  • • Take years to produce results, while shock evolves in minutes

As a result, modern medicine still lacks a validated, real-time model to predict, diagnose, and treat hemorrhagic shock in its earliest—and most critical—moments.

How Operation Lifeblood Is Changing the Future of Shock Care

Operation Lifeblood brings together military physicians, combat veterans, scientists, and engineers to break through the limits of traditional research and deliver lifesaving breakthroughs.

1. Cardiac Digital Twin for Hemorrhagic Shock

A first-of-its-kind, high-fidelity computational model of how the heart and circulatory system respond to blood loss—built using scientific machine learning, DoD trauma data, and advanced simulation.

This model will allow medics and clinicians to predict shock before it becomes fatal, test treatments safely in silico, and personalize resuscitation in real time.

2. Faster Translation From Battlefield to Hospital

Insights from digital twins are converted into deployable protocols, tools, and training for medics, first responders, and trauma surgeons—closing the gap between research and real-world lifesaving care.

3. A New Era of Trauma Innovation

Through competitive grants, partnerships, and open collaboration, Operation Lifeblood accelerates promising ideas that would otherwise never reach the warfighter or the civilian patient.

Trauma Medicine

Partnering with trauma centers and critical care teams to integrate digital twins and new standards of care at the bedside.

Our Mission Is Simple and Urgent
