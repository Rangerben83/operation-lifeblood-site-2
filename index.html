Mission & Vision

Mission

Service to those who serve — and everyone they protect.

The Problem

The Urgency of Hemorrhagic Shock

“Hemorrhage remains the leading cause of preventable death on the battlefield.”
— COL Brian Eastridge, MD, Joint Trauma System

Hemorrhagic shock happens when a person loses so much blood that the heart can’t pump enough to keep the brain and vital organs alive. Without rapid intervention— stopping the bleeding and restoring circulation—the body begins to shut down in minutes.

Why It Is the Leading Cause of Preventable Battlefield Death

On today’s battlefield, most deaths occur before the casualty reaches a surgeon. When blood loss is severe:

  • Bleeding can be internal and invisible.
  • Clots break apart during movement, flight, or stress.
  • Transfusion may only temporarily stabilize the patient.
  • Shock can return suddenly during MEDEVAC or early surgery.

Because of these challenges, the vast majority of potentially survivable combat deaths are caused by uncontrolled hemorrhage.

A Problem Recognized for Generations

From the American Civil War to Iraq and Afghanistan, battlefield surgeons have known the same truth: uncontrolled bleeding kills more Soldiers than any other battlefield injury.

Despite major advances in tourniquets, blood products, damage-control surgery, and rapid evacuation, hemorrhagic shock remains an unsolved problem — on the battlefield, in the ambulance, and in trauma centers around the world.

Why Traditional Research Hasn’t Solved It

Traditional clinical research is not designed for the speed, chaos, and ethical constraints of battlefield trauma:

  • It cannot ethically expose humans to life-threatening shock.
  • It cannot fully simulate battlefield or austere conditions.
  • It requires more standardized data than trauma patients can safely provide.
  • It moves in years, while shock evolves in minutes.

The result: modern medicine still lacks a validated, real-time model to predict, diagnose, and treat hemorrhagic shock in its earliest — and most critical — moments.

How We Work

A Functional Research Organization built for impact, not inertia.

Operation Lifeblood is structured as a Functional Research Organization (FRO): a dedicated, non-profit entity with a single, ambitious, and time-bounded mission. We assemble a full-time technical and clinical team, align them around clear milestones, and measure progress in real-world outcomes rather than publications alone.

01 – Time-Bound Mission

A 5–7 year horizon to move hemorrhagic shock from unsolved challenge to a domain with deployable tools, validated models, and new standards of care.

02 – Integrated Team

Cardiac surgeons, trauma physicians, combat medics, data scientists, and engineers working together — without institutional walls — toward one mission.

03 – Milestone-Driven

Clear technical, translational, and clinical milestones: validated models, decision-support tools, new protocols, and demonstration projects across military and civilian settings.

04 – Public Good Focus

Data, models, and guidelines developed as shared infrastructure for militaries, EMS systems, trauma centers, and researchers worldwide.

Core Programs

Five lines of effort, one mission.

1. Cardiac Digital Twin for Hemorrhagic Shock

A first-of-its-kind, high-fidelity computational model of how the heart and circulatory system respond to blood loss — built using scientific machine learning, DoD trauma data, and advanced simulation.

This cardiac digital twin will enable medics and clinicians to:

  • Predict shock before it becomes fatal.
  • Test treatments safely in silico.
  • Personalize resuscitation in real time.

2. Translational Medicine for First Responders & Civilian Trauma Care

Adapting battlefield-proven protocols, diagnostics, and resuscitation strategies to EMS, emergency departments, and trauma networks around the world — co-designed with frontline clinicians to fit real-world staffing, equipment, and training constraints.

3. Trauma Innovation Challenge Grants

Competitive grants that identify, de-risk, and accelerate bold ideas in shock reversal, resuscitation strategies, field diagnostics, and novel therapeutics. Successful concepts are integrated into the broader Lifeblood mission where they can scale and be evaluated.

4. Hemorrhagic Shock Data & Collaboration Network

A secure, ethically governed data commons connecting military, EMS, trauma centers, and research labs focused on hemorrhagic shock — enabling shared models, shared evidence, and shared learning, while respecting operational security and privacy.

5. Advocacy & Policy Engagement

Elevating hemorrhagic shock as a sustained funding and policy priority across defense, public health, and emergency-care systems, and aligning resources with the true scale of the problem.
