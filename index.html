MISSION

Service to those who serve — and everyone they protect.

Our mission is to eliminate preventable deaths from trauma and hemorrhagic shock by advancing innovative research, developing field-ready treatments, and rapidly translating military medical breakthroughs into lifesaving solutions for warfighters, first responders, and trauma patients worldwide.

We focus on the full continuum of care: from point of injury, through evacuation and resuscitation, to definitive treatment and recovery.

Vision

A world where survivable shock is no longer fatal.

We envision a world where no soldier or civilian dies from survivable shock—where science, technology, training, and systems of care work together to deliver rapid, effective, and deployable solutions in any environment.

The Problem

Survivable hemorrhagic shock still kills thousands every year.

Uncontrolled bleeding and shock remain leading causes of potentially preventable death after trauma. Despite decades of research and major advances in damage-control surgery, blood products, and evacuation, critical gaps persist between what we know, what we can do in the lab, and what medics and clinicians can reliably deliver under pressure.

On the Battlefield

Combat medics and corpsmen make life-and-death decisions with limited diagnostics, constrained supplies, and tactical threats.

  • • Delayed evacuation or denied MEDEVAC
  • • Unclear physiology in complex polytrauma
  • • Need for simple, robust, field-validated protocols

In Civilian Emergencies

EMS and emergency departments face unpredictable scenes, varying resources, and fragmented data.

  • • Long transport times and limited monitoring
  • • Variable access to blood products and ICU beds
  • • Gaps in translating military best practices to civilian systems

In Research & Development

Traditional funding and institutional structures excel at discovery, but often fall short on sustained, integrated translation.

  • • Short grants and siloed research efforts
  • • Limited incentives for building shared tools & data
  • • Slow movement from models to standard-of-care protocols

How We Work

A Functional Research Organization built for impact, not inertia.

Operation Lifeblood is structured as a Functional Research Organization (FRO): a dedicated, non-profit entity with a single, ambitious, and time-bounded mission. We assemble a full-time technical and clinical team, align them around clear milestones, and measure progress in real-world outcomes rather than publications alone.

01

Time-Bound Mission

A 5–7 year horizon to move hemorrhagic shock from unsolved challenge to a domain with deployable tools, validated models, and new standards of care.

02

Integrated Team

Cardiac surgeons, trauma physicians, combat medics, data scientists, and engineers working together—without institutional walls—toward one mission.

03

Milestone-Driven

Clear technical, translational, and clinical milestones: validated models, decision-support tools, new protocols, and demonstration projects across military and civilian settings.

04

Public Good Focus

Data, models, and guidelines developed as shared infrastructure for militaries, EMS systems, trauma centers, and researchers worldwide.

Core Programs

Five lines of effort, one mission.

Operation Lifeblood’s work is organized into five mutually reinforcing programs spanning advanced modeling, translational medicine, innovation funding, data infrastructure, and policy.

Cardiac Digital Twin for Hemorrhagic Shock

High-fidelity, patient-specific models of the heart and circulation to understand, predict, and guide treatment of hemorrhagic shock in real time.

From physiologic simulation to decision support for medics, EMS crews, and trauma teams operating in constrained, high-risk environments.

Translational Medicine for First Responders & Civilian Trauma Care

Adapting battlefield-proven protocols, diagnostics, and resuscitation strategies to EMS, emergency departments, and trauma networks around the world.

Co-designed with frontline clinicians to fit real-world staffing, equipment, and training constraints.

Trauma Innovation Challenge Grants

Competitive grants that identify, de-risk, and accelerate bold ideas in shock reversal, resuscitation strategies, field diagnostics, and novel therapeutics.

Successful concepts are integrated into the broader Lifeblood mission where they can scale and be evaluated.

Hemorrhagic Shock Data & Collaboration Network

A secure, ethically governed data commons connecting military, EMS, trauma centers, and research labs focused on hemorrhagic shock.

Enabling shared models, shared evidence, and shared learning, while respecting operational security and privacy.

Advocacy & Policy Engagement

Elevating hemorrhagic shock as a sustained funding and policy priority across defense, public health, and emergency-care systems.

Working with Congress, veterans' organizations, hospital systems, and international partners to align resources with the true scale of the problem.
