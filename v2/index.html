The Urgency of Hemorrhagic Shock

“Hemorrhage remains the leading cause of preventable death on the battlefield.”

— COL Brian Eastridge, MD, Joint Trauma System

What Is Hemorrhagic Shock?

Hemorrhagic shock happens when a person loses so much blood that the heart can’t pump enough to keep the brain and vital organs alive. Without rapid intervention—stopping the bleeding and restoring circulation—the body begins to shut down in minutes.

Why It Is the Leading Cause of Preventable Battlefield Death

On today’s battlefield, most deaths occur before the casualty reaches a surgeon. When blood loss is severe:

  • Bleeding can be internal and invisible.
  • Clots break apart during movement, flight, or stress.
  • Transfusion may only temporarily stabilize the patient.
  • Shock can return suddenly during MEDEVAC or early surgery.

Because of these challenges, the vast majority of potentially survivable combat deaths are caused by uncontrolled hemorrhage.

A Problem Recognized for Generations

150 YEARS OF KNOWING THE PROBLEM. STILL NO DEFINITIVE SOLUTION.

From the American Civil War to Iraq and Afghanistan, battlefield surgeons have known the same truth: uncontrolled bleeding kills more Soldiers than any other battlefield injury.

Despite major advances in tourniquets, blood products, damage-control surgery, and rapid evacuation, hemorrhagic shock remains an unsolved problem—on the battlefield, in the ambulance, and in trauma centers around the world.

Why Traditional Research Hasn’t Solved It

SHOCK EVOLVES IN MINUTES. MOST RESEARCH MOVES IN YEARS.

Conventional medical research relies on slow, linear clinical trials that are not designed for the chaos and ethical constraints of battlefield trauma:

  • They cannot ethically expose humans to life-threatening shock.
  • They can’t fully simulate battlefield or austere conditions.
  • They require more standardized data than trauma patients can safely provide.
  • They take years to produce results, while shock evolves in minutes.

The result: modern medicine still lacks a validated, real-time model to predict, diagnose, and treat hemorrhagic shock in its earliest—and most critical—moments.

How Operation Lifeblood Is Different

How Operation Lifeblood Is Changing the Future of Shock Care

THREE LINES OF INNOVATION. ONE SIMPLE, URGENT MISSION.

1. The Cardiac Digital Twin for Hemorrhagic Shock

A first-of-its-kind, high-fidelity computational model of how the heart and circulatory system respond to blood loss—built using scientific machine learning, DoD trauma data, and advanced simulation.

This digital twin will allow medics and clinicians to predict shock before it becomes fatal, test treatments safely in silico, and personalize resuscitation in real time.

2. Faster Translation from Battlefield to Hospital

Insights from digital twins are rapidly converted into deployable protocols, tools, and training for medics, first responders, and trauma surgeons—closing the gap between research and real-world lifesaving care.

Our goal is to adapt validated military shock-care innovations into civilian EMS and trauma networks within months, not decades.

3. A New Era of Trauma Innovation

Through competitive grants, partnerships, and open collaboration, Operation Lifeblood accelerates promising ideas that would otherwise never reach the warfighter or the civilian patient.

We prioritize projects that integrate with our digital twins, data networks, and translational pipelines to produce measurable, lifesaving impact.

Core Programs

FIVE PROGRAMS. ONE LIFEBLOOD MISSION.

Operation Lifeblood’s work is organized into five mutually reinforcing programs spanning advanced modeling, translational medicine, innovation funding, data infrastructure, and advocacy.

1. Cardiac Digital Twin for Hemorrhagic Shock

Develop a high-fidelity, multi-scale digital twin of the human heart and circulatory system to model, predict, and treat hemorrhagic shock in battlefield and austere conditions.

2. Translational Medicine for First Responders & Civilian Trauma Care

Adapt and deploy military trauma research findings for EMS, emergency departments, and disaster response teams worldwide.

3. Trauma Innovation Challenge Grants

Fund and accelerate promising early-stage projects aimed at overcoming irreversible shock, from novel therapeutics to field diagnostics.

4. Hemorrhagic Shock Data & Collaboration Network

Build the largest secure research and data-sharing platform dedicated to trauma and shock, connecting military, academic, and civilian centers.

5. Advocacy & Policy Engagement

Secure sustained funding and policy priority for hemorrhagic shock research at federal, state, and philanthropic levels.
